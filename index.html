Sno-King Hockey Webpage Redesign
Why redesign this site?

The Sno-King Hockey website is the homepage for the ice hockey association that I played at growing up, therefore it holds a special place in my heart, making its ugliness even more intolerable.

Let's improve this disaster of a webpage.

Problems with Current Site

The webpage contradicts the scan pattern of most users by placing action buttons on the left column rather than the bottom right corners. Additionally, the shapes of interactive elements such as buttons diverges from most users' mental models as they look more like view-only images. Therefore, novice users will have a hard time locating the links and pages they want to navigate to.

For casual users, the overuse of opaque images as backgrounds distracts from the texts and other relevant content on the website. This makes the site harder to memorize as users will associate content by the images rather than the text which displays more accurate info. The clustering of elements into poorly spaced spaces also reduces the memorability of the site as elements lack distinctive location and features.

The webpage lacks hierarchy by having three columns of content jammed horizontally next to each other. The congestion of content challenges even expert users as it makes it harder for them to locate the specific links that they are seeking and increases the likelihood of clicking on the wrong links, simply owing to the elements' proximity to each other.

The interactive elements on the page lacks contrast, making it difficult for visually impaired users to identify relevant content. Numerous images also lack alternative texts and forms lack labels, making it difficult for text readers to parse the webpage. Lastly, the HTML of the webpage contains empty links and headings that detract from the quality and readability of the source code.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Significant Changes
  • Interactive elements changed to be buttons rather than image blocks for the sake of clarity.
  • Replaced 3-column cluster with clearly separate sections that scroll down the page.
  • Rather than publishing all news equally, the headline news earns a magnified slot, the two secondary news get smaller slots, and the rest go on a news specific page.
  • Color scheme is simplified by using Sno-King's primary color, navy blue, as the main static color, relegating its secondary color, emerald green, to hover color.
  • Employs icons such as the magnifiying glass and menu button to reduce the number of statically displaced distractions.
